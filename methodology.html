How the roofing debris estimate works

The calculator is a planning tool for US asphalt-shingle tear-offs. It estimates material weight, converts that weight into a loose-volume range, and compares both with general dumpster size and weight ranges.

Weight assumptions

MaterialPlanning value
3-tab asphalt shingles240 lb per roofing square
Architectural asphalt shingles420 lb per roofing square
Roofing felt/underlayment15 lb per roofing square when selected

One roofing square covers 100 square feet. The shingle values come from GAF's general tear-off guidelines: 2,400 lb per 1,000 sq ft for three-tab shingles and 4,200 lb per 1,000 sq ft for architectural shingles. EPA methodology also uses approximately 250 lb per square as a general asphalt-shingle assumption.

Formula

roofing squares = roof area in square feet ÷ 100

base weight = squares × shingle weight per square × layers + selected extras

expected weight = base weight × (1 + planning allowance)

The displayed low/high range applies an additional field-condition range around the expected result. This acknowledges variation in product thickness, nails, flashing, dirt, moisture, and measurement quality.

Volume assumptions

Published volume-to-weight conversion factors for loose roofing-shingle debris vary. This calculator uses a range of 0.21–0.37 US tons per cubic yard and an expected value of 0.29 tons per cubic yard. The volume result is less certain than the material-weight result because packing, breakage, and loading practices matter greatly.

Limitations

  • Do not use the home footprint unless it accurately represents the roof surface area.
  • Tile, slate, metal, built-up roofing, and commercial membrane systems are not supported in version 1.
  • Decking removal is not automatically inferred. Add known extra weight manually.
  • The calculator does not verify local landfill rules, permits, truck limits, or provider-specific contracts.
  • The scale ticket and rental agreement determine the final charge.
